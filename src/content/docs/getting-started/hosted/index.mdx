## Neurodesk on HPC

Best for large-scale analyses and research projects. Available on many institutional HPC systems with high-performance computing capabilities, integrated with existing HPC infrastructure. Contact your local HPC support to check availability.

We have documented installations at several institutions:

- **University of Queensland** — [Bunya HPC](/getting-started/installations/bunya/) with full integration into UQ's research computing environment.
- **University of Michigan** — Great Lakes HPC, [installation guide and usage instructions](/getting-started/neurocommand/linux-and-hpc/#greatlakes---university-of-michigan).
- **Other institutions** — Many HPCs support Neurodesk through Singularity/Apptainer containers. Check our [HPC documentation](/getting-started/neurocommand/linux-and-hpc/) for general setup instructions, and work with your HPC administrators to enable Neurodesk support.

## Choosing the right option

Consider these factors when selecting a hosted solution:

1. **Resource requirements**: data processing needs, storage requirements, computing power needed.
2. **Access type**: browser-based access, SSH or command-line access, integration with existing workflows.
3. **Cost considerations**: free options (Neurodesk Play), institutional resources (HPC), cloud hosting costs.

## Getting support

Need help deciding which option is best for you?

- Check our [FAQ section](/overview/faq/).
- Join our [discussion forum](https://github.com/orgs/neurodesk/discussions).
- Contact your institutional IT support for HPC deployments.

## Security and data privacy

All hosted options prioritise secure data handling, user authentication, resource isolation, and compliance with institutional policies. Choose the option that best aligns with your institutional requirements and data governance policies.
